Cognitive AI

Machines that perceive, reason, and act

We build cognitive architectures for robots: a closed loop where perception feeds a world model, planners reason over it, skills execute, and field data improves the next release.

L1

Perception

Cameras, lidar, radar, and IMU streams fused into a structured, time-synchronized view of the scene.

L2

World model and memory

Persistent scene state, maps, and task memory, so the system reasons over what it knows, not just what it sees this frame.

L3

Reasoning and planning

LLM-based planners and behavior trees turn goals into constrained, verifiable action sequences with defined fallbacks.

L4

Skill execution

Typed skill interfaces on ROS 2 carry plans to actuators, with runtime monitoring and safe abort paths.

L5

Learning loop

Field data flows back through evaluation harnesses and regression suites into the next model and planner release.

Delivery Model

A collaboration model built on proof

We earn scope step by step. Each phase produces something you can evaluate before committing further.

Phase 1

Discovery

Technical workshops to map your system, constraints, and goals. Output: a scoped proposal with architecture, milestones, and acceptance criteria.

Phase 2

Pilot

A bounded engagement, typically six to twelve weeks, delivering a working component against the agreed criteria. You judge the result, not the pitch.

Phase 3

Integration

The pilot component becomes part of your product: interface hardening, test coverage, documentation, and joint reviews with your engineers.

Phase 4

Scale

A dedicated team grows with your roadmap, with stable points of contact, agreed SLAs, and quarterly planning aligned to your release cycle.
